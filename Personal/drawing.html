Comic Narrative: The Encounter

I was deeply touched by the song lyric of “Homeword Bound”. A lonely traveler was sitting in the railway station with his suitcase and guitar in hands. I wanted to tell a story about a lonely traveler who came to an unfamiliar city. I made the story with a happy ending to show my good wishes to my life.

The Corridor

This is a drawing with pencils and charcoals depicting a beautiful corridor in Xiamen University, China. I mimicked Georges Seurat’s soft and relaxed drawing technique to create the atmosphere rather than the object itself.

On The Seashore

This is a drawing with pencils and charcoals which shows the scenery on the seashore in Vancouver. Claude Monet loved to observe the beauty of nature especially the water. I mimicked Monet’s loose handling of objects. With different charcoals, I can create different fragmented thick brush strokes to make the sea look shining and flowing.

Summer Afternoon

Having learned the art style and technique of Paul Cézanne, I drew the two buildings pitching to the right and painted the shadows of windows, doors and walls illustrating that the light is coming from the left side. Then I added a big tree on the left in order to gain the balance. There are hatched shadows under trees and bushes creating the feeling of dimension.
